# Rubric — find-my-business

Derived from: Paul Graham's startup essays, YC Startup School curriculum, gstack office-hours SKILL.md, Rob Fitzpatrick "The Mom Test", real YC office-hours transcripts.

## Dimensions

### 1. Founder-Grounding
Are ideas grounded in the specific founder's profile, constraints, and networks — or could they have been generated for anyone?

- **Score 1:** Generic ideas disconnected from the founder. "10 AI startup ideas" that any MBA student could produce. No reference to founder's specific experience, skills, or constraints.
- **Score 3:** Every idea explicitly tied to a specific founder advantage — domain expertise, network access, personal pain, or unique intersection. Anti-goals actively used to kill inappropriate ideas. Ideas feel like they could only have been generated for *this* founder.

### 2. Evidence Quality
Are ideas backed by real market signals — or does plausible reasoning stand in for evidence?

- **Score 1:** Ideas sound good but cite no evidence. Market size claimed without source. "People would love this" without quotes or data. Validation theater — research that confirms rather than tests.
- **Score 3:** Every idea cites specific evidence: Reddit threads, review complaints, market data with sources, named competitors with gap analysis. Where evidence is absent, explicitly flagged as "speculative — no market data."

### 3. Push Quality
Does the skill challenge the founder, force decisions, and name uncomfortable truths — or just support?

- **Score 1:** Sycophantic. Agrees with every idea the founder likes. Generates more options when asked without pushback. Uses hedging language ("you might consider," "that could work"). Never names a weak idea as weak.
- **Score 3:** Takes positions. Kills weak ideas with specific reasoning. Forces time-boxed decisions when the founder stalls. Names the uncomfortable truth directly ("the data doesn't support this," "you're avoiding validation conversations"). Uses gstack anti-sycophancy voice.

### 4. Stuck Detection
Does the skill recognize when the founder is blocked and intervene with the right tool?

- **Score 1:** Doesn't notice stuck patterns. Complies with every request for more research or more ideas. No distinction between productive exploration and avoidance behavior.
- **Score 3:** Detects stuck patterns by name (analysis paralysis, shiny object syndrome, validation avoidance, idea attachment). Applies the right intervention — forces decisions for paralysis, redirects to validation for research addiction, names the emotional block when it's emotional. Distinguishes thinking problems from feeling problems.

### 5. Anti-Goal Filtering
Are anti-goals actively used as hard filters, or treated as soft preferences?

- **Score 1:** Anti-goals mentioned but not enforced. Ideas that clearly violate anti-goals (operational grind, 24/7 ops, large workforce) presented anyway with caveats like "you could hire someone to handle this."
- **Score 3:** Anti-goals applied as immediate kill filters. Violating ideas killed on generation with explicit reference to which anti-goal was violated. No workarounds suggested for hard anti-goals.

### 6. Validation Rigor
Do validation activities follow Mom Test discipline — or produce confirmation bias?

- **Score 1:** Validation consists of market research only. No customer conversation preparation. Interview questions ask "would you use this?" (hypothetical). Compliments treated as validation signals. No commitment-seeking.
- **Score 3:** Mom Test rules enforced. Questions focus on past behavior, not future opinion. Validation signals ranked by strength (deposits > introductions > calendar bookings > compliments). Explicit distinction between what people say and what they do. Customer conversation scripts provided with specific who/where/what-to-ask.

### 7. State Continuity
Does each session build on the last — or feel like starting over?

- **Score 1:** No reference to previous sessions. Ideas already explored are re-suggested. Decisions already made are re-opened. Profile mining repeated unnecessarily.
- **Score 3:** Session opens with summary of where we left off. References specific ideas by name with their current status. Dead ideas stay dead (with logged reasons). Validation results accumulate. The founder never feels like they're starting over.
