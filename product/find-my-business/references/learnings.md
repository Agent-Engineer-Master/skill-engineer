# Learnings — find-my-business

## What Has Worked

- Asking "what did you actually build?" (not just "what did you work on?") unlocks more honest capability assessment than any framework question
- Premise challenges before validation sprints saved time — stopped wasting sessions researching ideas that had structural problems
- Letting the founder name a dead-end category himself rather than dismissing it created space to revisit it with a better model later

## What Has Failed

- Generic idea generation before checking prior research — always check for any prior exploration work or research the founder has already done before generating ideas. Half a session can be skipped.

## Patterns and Preferences

- Founders often think in parallel paths naturally — support this rather than forcing convergence too early. Some founders move from "one acquisition" to "maybe a rollup" to "two live paths" in one session.
- Anti-sycophancy works — direct pushback on specific red flags (e.g. website down, single SKU, Q4 concentration) is received positively. Keep this up.
- Creative deal structures (royalty/licensing, earn-out, minority stake) often come from the founder, not the skill — watch for them when a business looks distressed; don't default to outright acquisition or walk away as the only two options.

## Open Questions

- Does the skill need a specific "acquisition evaluation" sub-phase? The current phases (profile → ideation → validation → deep-validation → commitment) don't map cleanly onto evaluating multiple live deals simultaneously.
