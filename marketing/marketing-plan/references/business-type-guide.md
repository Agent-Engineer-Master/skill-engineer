## SaaS / Software Products

### How the ICP discovers solutions
Primarily via search (bottom-funnel: "best [tool] for [use case]"), peer recommendations in Slack/Discord communities, and increasingly via AI search (GEV).

### Recommended channel sequence by stage

**Pre-launch (no customers):**
- Create: founder LinkedIn/X content around the problem space (not the product)
- Convert: waitlist with a clear value exchange (early access, pricing lock, exclusives)
- Do NOT: run paid ads to an unvalidated landing page

**Early (1-50 customers):**
- Capture: SEO on 3-5 bottom-funnel keywords ("[your category] for [ICP]", "best [alternative] alternative")
- Create: case studies and founder story content
- Convert: high-touch onboarding (personal Zoom calls with every customer)
- GEV: publish "X vs Y" comparison content for top 3 competitors

**Growing (50+ customers):**
- Capture: expand SEO, test paid search on validated keywords
- Create: continue founder content, add customer-generated content
- Convert: automate onboarding, add in-app referral mechanism
- GEV: pursue press/publication citations, guest appearances on podcasts

### Primary channel recommendation
Bottom-funnel SEO + founder-led content on LinkedIn/X. These compound over time and don't require a minimum budget.

### Budget allocation defaults

| Stage | Content + SEO | Paid | Email/CRM | Experimental |
|-------|-------------|------|-----------|-------------|
| Pre-launch | 70% (founder time) | 0% | 20% | 10% |
| Early | 50% | 10% (search only) | 25% | 15% |
| Growing | 35% | 25% | 20% | 20% |

### Common failure modes
1. **Product-first content**: writing about features instead of outcomes. Fix: lead with customer problem.
2. **Too many pricing tiers at launch**: confuses buyers and complicates positioning. Fix: one or two tiers max.
3. **Free tier before PMF**: masks retention signals. Free users don't behave like paying customers.
4. **Skipping sales calls**: early founders avoid talking to customers. Each call is market research.
5. **Paid ads before organic validation**: CAC is unknowable until you have organic signal.

### Key metrics to track
- Activation rate (% of signups who complete core action)
- Time-to-first-value (minutes to activation)
- 30-day retention
- Expansion MRR (upsells, seat additions)

## DTC Ecommerce (Dropship or Own Inventory)

### How the ICP discovers solutions
Via social feeds (Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest), search (category + product keywords), and Amazon/marketplace search. Gifting and unboxing content drives significant awareness for lifestyle products.

### Recommended channel sequence by stage

**Pre-launch (no customers):**
- Create: lifestyle content on TikTok/Instagram showing product in use — do not reveal the brand name yet, test creative angles first
- Capture: organic Pinterest and SEO for category keywords
- Convert: waitlist or pre-order page with social proof (even mock-ups)
- Do NOT: run Meta/TikTok ads to a new account with no creative history (algorithm needs 50+ conversions to optimise)

**Early (1-50 customers):**
- Capture: Google Shopping (if inventory confirmed) + Meta with 3-5 creative variants
- Create: seed micro-influencers (1k-50k followers) with product for organic UGC
- Convert: post-purchase email sequence (review ask -> upsell -> referral)
- GEV: product structured data + category comparison content ("best [product type] for [occasion/use case]")

**Growing (50+ customers):**
- Capture: scale paid ads on proven creatives, add Google Search
- Create: scale influencer program, add brand ambassador tier
- Convert: SMS/loyalty program, subscription offer if applicable
- Retention: repeat purchase rate is the north star — add 30/60/90-day re-engagement flows

### Primary channel recommendation
Paid social (Meta/TikTok) once creatives are validated + organic short-form video. SEO is too slow for initial DTC traction; use it as a compounding layer from day one.

### Budget allocation defaults

| Stage | Content + SEO | Paid social | Email/SMS | Influencers | Tools |
|-------|-------------|------------|-----------|------------|-------|
| Pre-launch | 40% (founder time) | 0% | 20% | 30% (product seeding) | 10% |
| Early | 20% | 35% | 20% | 15% | 10% |
| Growing | 15% | 40% | 20% | 15% | 10% |

### Common failure modes
1. **Launching ads before confirming supplier inventory**: overselling causes refunds and kills trust.
2. **One creative angle**: if the one ad stops working, revenue stops. Build 5+ creative angles in parallel.
3. **Ignoring email/SMS**: paid acquisition is rented. Email list is owned. Build from day one.
4. **Competing on price**: margin gets destroyed. Compete on ICP fit, not price.
5. **Wide product range at launch**: 1-3 hero SKUs max. Confusing range = low conversion.
6. **Running ads without pixel data**: Meta/TikTok pixels need 50+ purchase events to learn. Budget for the learning phase.

### Key metrics to track
- ROAS (return on ad spend) — target >3x in early phase
- CPP (cost per purchase)
- Repeat purchase rate at 90 days
- AOV (average order value)
- LTV:CAC ratio

## Service Businesses (Online Delivery)

Covers: consulting, coaching, fractional services, agencies, productised services, online courses.

### How the ICP discovers solutions
Primarily via referrals and trusted recommendations, LinkedIn/X search for practitioner expertise, and communities where the ICP spends time. Cold outbound is more effective here than in SaaS or DTC.

### Recommended channel sequence by stage

**Pre-launch (no customers):**
- Create: demonstrate expertise through LinkedIn case studies, frameworks, and POVs — not promotional content
- Convert: direct outreach to 20-30 targeted ICP contacts (warm, not cold — use existing network first)
- Do NOT: build a website before getting first client. Waste of time pre-PMF.

**Early (1-10 clients):**
- Create: case studies from every engagement (with permission). Post on LinkedIn with specifics.
- Capture: LinkedIn profile optimised for ICP search terms. Request referrals actively.
- Convert: define the exact engagement structure and outcome (scope creep is a GTM problem)
- GEV: contribute to publications and communities where the ICP asks questions

**Growing (10+ clients):**
- Capture: content SEO for specific problem-solution terms. Podcast appearances.
- Create: build own content distribution (newsletter, podcast, YouTube)
- Convert: referral program with existing clients. Partnership with adjacent service providers.

### Primary channel recommendation
Founder-led LinkedIn content + direct network activation. Services businesses are sold through trust — content builds trust at scale, network activates it immediately.

### Budget allocation defaults

| Stage | Content (founder time) | Outreach tools | CRM/Email | Networking | Experimental |
|-------|----------------------|---------------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Pre-launch | 60% | 15% | 10% | 10% | 5% |
| Early | 45% | 10% | 15% | 15% | 15% |
| Growing | 30% | 5% | 20% | 20% | 25% |

### Common failure modes
1. **Generalist positioning**: "I help businesses grow" attracts nobody. Niche down to one ICP + one outcome.
2. **No social proof before outreach**: cold outreach without case studies has near-zero conversion.
3. **Productising too late**: custom-scoped projects don't scale. Package into 2-3 defined offers early.
4. **Underpricing**: low prices signal low value in services. Price is a positioning signal.
5. **Relying on one client**: 50%+ revenue concentration is a GTM single point of failure.

### Key metrics to track
- Revenue per client
- Client retention / renewal rate
- Pipeline conversion rate (lead -> proposal -> closed)
- Referral rate (% of new clients from existing client referrals)
- Utilisation rate (if time-based delivery)

## Marketplaces and Platforms

Covers: two-sided marketplaces, platform products, aggregator businesses.

### The cold-start problem
Marketplaces have a chicken-and-egg problem: supply won't come without demand; demand won't come without supply. The GTM must sequence supply-side vs demand-side activation.

### Recommended approach
1. **Solve the supply side first** — recruit 10-20 high-quality supply-side participants before launch. Constrain supply artificially to create scarcity and maintain quality bar.
2. **Activate demand side via Create** — build demand-side audience through content before opening the marketplace.
3. **Focus on liquidity** — a marketplace is healthy when >70% of listed supply transacts within 30 days. Optimise for this before growing either side.

### Budget allocation defaults

| Stage | Community building | Content | Paid (demand-side) | Tools | Supply-side incentives |
|-------|-------------------|---------|-------------------|-------|----------------------|
| Pre-launch | 40% | 30% | 0% | 10% | 20% |
| Early | 25% | 25% | 15% | 10% | 25% |
| Growing | 15% | 20% | 30% | 10% | 25% |

### Common failure modes
1. Growing both sides simultaneously — spreads attention, prevents liquidity
2. Wrong monetisation timing — charging before liquidity creates trust deficit
3. Geographic spread — go deep in one city/vertical before expanding

## Personal Brand Flywheel Modifier

Apply this section when Q7 (intake) reveals the founder has an existing audience (LinkedIn, X, newsletter, community) that overlaps with the ICP.

### When it applies
Founder has 500+ followers on a platform where the ICP spends time, OR has a newsletter/community with direct ICP access. Even a small, targeted audience (500-2k) is more valuable than a large irrelevant one.

### How it changes channel recommendations
- **Personal brand becomes primary Capture + Create channel** — not a supporting channel. Product appears as proof of expertise, not as the subject of promotional posts.
- **Paid acquisition becomes even less necessary** — organic authority is already partially built. The constraint shifts from "build an audience" to "activate the audience you have."
- **The sales cycle compresses** — ICP already trusts the founder before the first purchase interaction. First-customer motion relies on activation, not cold outreach.

### The flywheel structure
```
Founder posts about the problem space (not the product)
  -> builds topical authority with ICP
  -> ICP trusts founder as practitioner, not marketer
  -> product appears as what the founder built to solve it
  -> ICP converts because trust was already deposited
  -> satisfied customer shares in community
  -> new ICP member enters via community mention
  -> loop compounds brand authority each lap
```

### Content integration rules
- Product mentions should appear as evidence of the founder's expertise, not as advertisements
- One explicit product post per month maximum — all other mentions are embedded in problem/craft content
- Case studies and build logs are the highest-converting format: they show the work, which is the trust deposit
- Never put promotional copy in the post body — put the intake/purchase link in the first comment

### Channel priority shift when flywheel is active
| Without personal brand | With personal brand flywheel |
|---|---|
| SEO as primary (long ramp) | Personal brand as primary (immediate) |
| Cold outreach as first-customer motion | Warm audience activation as first-customer motion |
| Community seeding from scratch | Community presence already partially established |
| Brand trust must be built before conversion | Partial trust already banked — conversion threshold lower |

### Budget modifier when flywheel is active
- Shift 20-30% of budget allocation from paid acquisition to content production (design, editing, scheduling tools)
- Reduce experimental budget to 5% — the personal brand IS the experiment that's already validated
- Increase email/CRM allocation by 10% — convert followers to owned list faster

## Universal Rules

Apply to all business types:

1. **One primary channel at launch** — diluting across 5 channels produces nothing. Commit to one for 60-90 days before evaluating.
2. **Owned > rented audiences** — email list and SEO compound. Social following can be cut off. Build owned from day one regardless of primary channel.
3. **Paid ads after organic signal** — if you can't get 10 customers organically, paid won't fix the problem. Organic validates positioning; paid amplifies it.
4. **Activation before acquisition** — if you don't know what your activation moment is and can't measure it, don't open paid acquisition. You'll pour money into a leaky bucket.
5. **Dark social is real** — a significant portion of buyer decisions happen in private channels (Slack communities, DMs, group chats) that aren't tracked. Reddit, peer Slack groups, and private Discord servers are now primary research channels for B2B buyers. Invest in community presence 6-12 months before you need it.
6. **Budget the 70/20/10 rule** — 70% proven channels, 20% experimental, 10% tools. See `execution-playbooks.md` for stage-specific tiers.
7. **Retention from day one** — deploy welcome series, activation nudge, and win-back campaigns before scaling acquisition. See `execution-playbooks.md` (retention campaigns section).
