# Worked Example — AG1 (athleticgreens.com)

Illustrative only. AG1 chosen because it is a well-documented DTC brand with public signal across every section. Use this as a model for tone, depth, and citation discipline — not as a source of real numbers.

## §0 Research confidence & source map

**Confidence:** Medium-high. Sources accessed: site, Meta Ad Library, ImportYeti, SimilarWeb, Trustpilot, Reddit, LinkedIn, Google News, Charm.io free tier, BuiltWith. Paywalled / not accessed: PitchBook, Similarweb pro tier. Estimated (not confirmed): revenue, CAC, supply chain concentration.

## §1 Executive summary

- Single-SKU daily greens powder at $79/month subscription, positioned as "foundational nutrition" for health-conscious professionals.
- ~600M+ estimated annual revenue per 2023 TechCrunch reporting; ~$1.2B valuation at 2022 raise [1].
- Moat is brand + podcast sponsorship dominance, not product differentiation — category has 40+ credible alternatives.
- Unit economics are likely healthy: $79 AOV, subscription-led, ~65% gross margin (category benchmark).
- **One thing to know:** AG1's entire edifice rests on paid podcast attribution that post-iOS14 nobody can cleanly measure. If blended CAC has degraded and they haven't shifted to MMM, the economics may already be broken.

## §2 Brand & positioning

Founded 2010 by Chris Ashenden. Positioning: "one product, done right." Voice: clinical, confident, founder-led. Archetype: The Sage. Defensibility: brand recall in a category where most competitors are indistinguishable, not formulation. [2][3]

## §3 Product & value proposition

One SKU (AG1 powder), sold as 30-day pouches or single-serve packets. Price ladder: $79/mo subscribe, $99 one-time. JTBD: "I want to feel like I've covered my nutritional bases without thinking about it." Evidence from Reddit `r/Supplements`: ~60% of threads mention "convenience" or "one thing" as the value [4].

## §4 Market & category

Functional greens / powdered supplements. Bottom-up TAM: ~8M US health-conscious subscribers × $950/yr average = ~$7.6B addressable. Statista puts broader "functional nutrition" at $68B globally (2024) [5] — treat as ceiling. Tailwinds: continued wellness consumer growth. Headwinds: FDA scrutiny of supplement claims, manufacturer-direct competition from Chinese private label on TikTok Shop.

## §6 Pricing & unit economics

- AOV (subscribe): $79
- Est. COGS: ~$15–20 per pouch (~est., method: supplement category benchmark 20–25% of retail, per Luca data + raw-material spot check)
- CM1 (gross margin): ~75–80% `~ (est.)`
- Fulfillment + shipping: ~$6/order
- CM2: ~$55 = ~70%
- Est. new-customer CAC: $80–130 (Eightx supplements benchmark; Meta Ad Library shows 47+ active creatives with an average run of 34 days, suggesting efficient creative cycle)
- CM3: ~ negative on first purchase; positive from month 2
- Payback: ~2 months on subscription

**Caveat:** all of the above assumes subscription retention at ~70% month-1 and ~85% thereafter. AG1 does not disclose churn; TechCrunch reporting hints at strong LTV but this is not confirmed.

## §15 Investor verdict

**Bull case:**
- Category-defining brand in a $68B global category.
- Subscription-led revenue with best-in-class CAC payback.
- Podcast moat is hard to replicate without AG1's 2014–2022 head start.

**Bear case:**
- Product is undifferentiated; defensibility is pure marketing spend.
- Post-iOS14 attribution makes the CAC number the entire company runs on structurally unreliable.
- Manufacturer-direct private label on TikTok Shop at $25/mo is an active threat to the $79 price point.

**What would change my mind:** Investable below ~$1.5B valuation if audited CAC payback <3 months and net revenue retention >110%. Unattractive above $2B without demonstrated defensibility beyond brand. Would reconsider bull case if AG1 ships a second SKU that recruits new customers on a different JTBD.
