# Discontinuities Catalog

A **discontinuity** is a non-smooth event or trend that breaks linear extrapolation of the industry trajectory. Three types — scan all three; do not stop at the obvious one.

> Note: Christensen's "disruption" (2015 narrowing) is a SPECIFIC sub-type — low-end or new-market entrant displacing incumbent. Senior practice in 2026 uses broader "discontinuity" language to include regulatory and behavioral shifts that disruption-theory doesn't cover.

## The three types

### 1. Regulatory discontinuities
Policy / standard / permit / mandate shifts that change the cost structure, the allowed activity set, or the competitive boundary.

Examples (2026 active):
- EU AI Act enforcement (Aug 2026 high-risk system requirements)
- Section 174 R&D capitalization unwinding in US tax code
- GLP-1 reclassification or coverage mandate changes
- EU CSRD scope expansions
- FDA 510(k) AI/ML SaMD pathway updates
- State-level PFAS bans cascading to product reformulation

### 2. Technology discontinuities
Capability or cost step-change in an underlying technology that the industry depends on.

Examples (2026 active):
- LLM inference cost falling another 5-10x by 2027
- Battery $/kWh crossing the $80 threshold (EV TCO parity)
- mRNA / cell-therapy manufacturing cost halving
- Ambient compute / on-device LLMs displacing cloud inference
- Quantum-resistant cryptography mandates by 2030

### 3. Behavioral discontinuities
Customer preference or use-pattern shifts that change demand shape, channel mix, or willingness-to-pay.

Examples (2026 active):
- AI-mediated shopping (agentic commerce) shifting category buyer
- GLP-1 demand suppression in snacks/alcohol/QSR
- Return-to-office reversal or entrenchment
- Subscription fatigue → re-bundling
- Gen-Z preference for AI-narrated entertainment

## Timing window methodology

**MANDATORY: explicit year ranges.** "Soon" / "eventually" / "in coming years" fail validation.

Structure each timing window as:

| Discontinuity | Likely window | Possible window | Leading indicator |
|---------------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| [name] | YYYY-YYYY | YYYY-YYYY | [named signal that resolves the window] |

The **leading indicator** is the signal you would track to know whether the window is opening or sliding. Examples:
- For EU AI Act: "first enforcement action against a major US AI provider"
- For battery cost: "Tesla or CATL announcing <$80/kWh cell ASP"
- For agentic commerce: "Shopify or Amazon publishing share of orders placed by agents"

## Direction of impact

Each discontinuity acts on the industry profit pool in one of three ways:

- **Compress** — overall profit pool shrinks (price erosion, cost increase, demand suppression)
- **Expand** — overall profit pool grows (new use cases, regulatory tailwind, demographic shift)
- **Redistribute** — profit migrates between layers or players (intermediary disintermediated, new entrant captures value, layer shifts)

State direction explicitly for each discontinuity.

## How many to identify

≥2 across the three types for a 5yr horizon. Real industries in 2026 typically have 3-5. If your scan returns only one, your scan was incomplete — re-scan all three types deliberately.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ "AI will disrupt this industry soon" — no type, no window, no leading indicator
- ❌ Only technology discontinuities considered (regulatory and behavioral skipped)
- ❌ Timing window stated as "in the coming years" or "by the end of the decade"
- ❌ Direction of impact not stated (compress vs expand vs redistribute)
- ❌ Leading indicator absent — the discontinuity is unfalsifiable
