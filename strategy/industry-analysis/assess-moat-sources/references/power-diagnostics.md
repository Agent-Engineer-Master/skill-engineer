# Per-Power Diagnostic Questions

For each of the 7 Powers, answer these diagnostics before marking **present / absent / nascent**. Each present-Power assertion in the output must reference a passing diagnostic.

## 1. Scale Economies — diagnostic

- What is the minimum efficient scale (MES) in this industry / stage? (Express in $ revenue or unit volume.)
- What is the cost penalty (unit cost % gap) for a player at 50% of MES?
- Among new entrants over the last 5 years, how many reached MES? If none — why?
- Is the cost curve still declining at the largest player's scale, or has it flattened?

**Present test:** MES is large relative to feasible entrant scale AND sub-MES cost penalty is binding (>5% margin compression).

## 2. Network Economies — diagnostic

- Same-side or cross-side network effect? (Buyer-buyer, seller-seller, buyer-seller.)
- Does utility per user increase monotonically with N? (Plot if possible.)
- Is HHI rising over time? Is the #1 player's share increasing?
- Could a niche entrant seed liquidity and grow out? (If yes → network effect is weak / local.)
- Is there a multi-homing tax? (Users on multiple networks → weaker lock-in.)

**Present test:** documented utility curve AND winner-take-most concentration trend AND failed niche-out attempts.

## 3. Counter-Positioning — diagnostic

- Name the incumbent who CANNOT copy. What asset would copying cannibalise?
- Has the incumbent tried and failed, or visibly avoided?
- Is the entrant's new model structurally cheaper or better — quantified?
- What is the incumbent's plausible response window? (Once they DO copy, Counter-Positioning expires.)

**Present test:** named incumbent with named cannibalisation asset + evidence of avoidance OR failed copy attempt.

## 4. Switching Costs — diagnostic

- Estimate the customer's switching cost in $ per account AND hours of internal effort.
- What is the typical contract length / lock-in mechanism?
- What % of new revenue comes from net-new logos vs. expansion in the install base?
- What is the gross churn rate? (Below 5% annual usually signals real switching costs.)
- Are switching costs financial (real $), procedural (re-implementation effort), or relational (sunk-cost / training)? Which?

**Present test:** quantified switching cost > 1× annual contract value OR gross churn <5% AND >70% revenue from existing logos.

## 5. Branding — diagnostic

- Is there a measurable price premium vs an objectively-identical generic offering? (Quantify in %.)
- How long did the brand take to build? Decades → durable; <5 years → fragile.
- Is the brand category-defining or interchangeable with 2-3 peers? (Top-of-mind unprompted recall.)
- Does the brand carry trust/safety associations OR information-asymmetry associations? (Latter erodes faster under AI.)

**Present test:** quantified price premium >10% on identical-spec product AND >10-year build history.

## 6. Cornered Resource — diagnostic

- Name the resource. Name the legal / contractual / geographical / scarcity mechanism that corners it.
- How long does the cornering last? (Patent expiry, lease term, regulatory review cycle, depletion rate.)
- Could a well-funded competitor licence, acquire, or substitute equivalent access? At what cost?
- Is access preferential AND on attractive terms (not just exclusive)?

**Present test:** named resource + named mechanism + cornering duration > horizon OR multi-year remaining OR substitution-cost-prohibitive.

## 7. Process Power — diagnostic

- Name the specific process — NOT "operational excellence" but the named routine (e.g., Toyota's andon-cord-driven kaizen loop; ASML's vendor-managed inventory).
- How many years has the process compounded?
- Has any competitor poached senior leaders from the holder? Did the process transfer? (No transfer = evidence of Process Power.)
- Is the process tacit (embedded in routines / culture) or codified (documented and licensable)?

**Present test:** named process + multi-year compounding + failed replication attempt (poaching or licensing) within the last 5 years.

## Nascent vs Present vs Absent

| State | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **Present** | Power exists today; benefit + barrier both demonstrable. |
| **Nascent** | Conditions exist for Power to emerge within horizon, but benefit + barrier not yet both binding. |
| **Absent** | One or both of benefit / barrier missing AND no credible path within horizon. |

The output must mark all 7 Powers in one of these three states. None silently omitted.

## Anti-patterns (REJECTED)

The validator rejects any Power assertion whose justification uses these phrases as load-bearing:

- "first-mover" (alone)
- "differentiation" / "differentiated"
- "adds value" / "value-added"
- "is critical" / "mission-critical"
- "expertise" (alone)
- "relationships" (alone)
- "high quality" / "superior product"
- "complexity" (alone)
- "operational excellence" / "execution" (alone)

Pair these with a named Power if they are part of the evidence chain — never let them stand alone.
