# Anti-Patterns — Common Power Mis-Identifications

The validator REJECTS any output that uses these patterns as a load-bearing Power claim. Each anti-pattern below shows the wrong framing, the diagnosis, and the corrected framing.

## 1. "Operational excellence" labelled as Process Power

- **Wrong:** "Acme has Process Power because they have operational excellence in manufacturing."
- **Diagnosis:** "Operational excellence" is a description, not a process. Process Power requires (a) a NAMED routine, (b) multi-year compounding, (c) evidence of failed replication.
- **Right:** "Acme has Process Power in their vendor-managed-inventory routine, compounded since 2008; competitors poached two ops VPs in 2020-2022 and could not replicate the inventory turn premium [V: Acme 10-K + competitor 10-K]."

## 2. "First-mover" as a standalone Power

- **Wrong:** "Beta will hold a moat because they are the first-mover."
- **Diagnosis:** First-mover is a position, not a Power. The question is WHICH Power the first-mover position enabled.
- **Right:** "Beta's first-mover position enabled them to seed liquidity on both sides of the marketplace, establishing Network Economies that have compounded as HHI rose from 0.12 to 0.34 over five years [C: industry HHI series]."

## 3. "Differentiation" as a Power

- **Wrong:** "Gamma is protected because their product is highly differentiated."
- **Diagnosis:** Differentiation is the symptom; the question is WHY the differentiation persists. What benefit + barrier sustains it?
- **Right:** "Gamma sustains differentiation via a Cornered Resource (exclusive 12-year supply contract with the only US-domiciled rare-earth processor [V: 10-K Note 14])."

## 4. "Brand recognition" without price premium

- **Wrong:** "Delta has Branding Power — everyone knows the name."
- **Diagnosis:** Recognition is necessary but not sufficient. Branding-as-Power requires (a) measurable price premium on objectively-identical product, (b) multi-year build.
- **Right:** "Delta's brand commands a 22% price premium vs private-label equivalents in blind shelf tests [V: Delta consumer panel 2024], built over 35 years of consistent positioning."

## 5. "Patents" without expiry framing

- **Wrong:** "Epsilon's patent portfolio gives them Cornered Resource Power."
- **Diagnosis:** Patents are Cornered Resource ONLY for the remaining patent term. Durability assessment requires expiry dates.
- **Right:** "Epsilon's core patent expires Q2 2028 [V: USPTO record]. Cornered Resource is therefore Short-duration (<3y). Pipeline patents extend protection on adjacent molecules but the core erodes."

## 6. "Customer lock-in" without quantification

- **Wrong:** "Zeta enjoys high switching costs."
- **Diagnosis:** Lock-in language is vague. Switching Costs require quantified switching cost in $ or hours AND a churn metric.
- **Right:** "Zeta's switching cost averages $480k per customer (re-implementation effort) — 1.7× annual contract value [I: industry consultant benchmark]. Gross churn is 3.1% annual [V: Zeta IPO prospectus]."

## 7. "Adds value" / "is critical" / "high quality"

- **Wrong:** "The integration stage captures profit because it adds the most value."
- **Diagnosis:** Tautological. Every stage adds value — that is what "stage" means. "Critical" describes importance, not mechanism. "Quality" is competitive.
- **Right:** Name the Helmer Power. If no Power identifiable, state "not structurally protected — expect arbitrage by [named entrant] within [horizon]."

## 8. "Expertise" / "relationships" without specification

- **Wrong:** "Our team's deep expertise and trusted client relationships form the moat."
- **Diagnosis:** Vague. Expertise → Process Power or Cornered Resource (talent)? Relationships → Switching Costs or Branding?
- **Right:** "The relationships represent Switching Costs — 8-year average client tenure with embedded workflow integration; gross churn 4% [V: management]. Expertise concentration in 12 senior partners is a Cornered Resource (talent) but is fragile — 3 left in 2024 [C: trade press]."

## 9. Omitted Powers

- **Wrong:** Output discusses Scale and Network Economies in detail; says nothing about the other 5.
- **Diagnosis:** Silent omission is the most common bias — writer focuses on present Powers and skips absent ones. But absence is also load-bearing (it tells the reader what the winner cannot rely on).
- **Right:** All 7 Powers explicitly marked **present / absent / nascent** with one-line evidence each.

## 10. Cross-skill drift

- **Wrong:** value-chain-profit-pools.md names "branding" as the protection for the highest-EBIT stage; moat-sources.md names "switching costs" as the dominant Power for the same industry — without reconciliation.
- **Diagnosis:** Sub-skills written independently, no consistency check.
- **Right:** step-6 reconciliation explicitly compares the two skills' Power claims. Mismatch surfaced with proposed resolution. Block Gate 2 until reconciled.

## 11. AI dismissed in one line

- **Wrong:** "AI does not affect the moat."
- **Diagnosis:** Helmer's own 2024-26 commentary is that AI asymmetrically affects Switching Costs, Branding, and Scale Economies. Dismissive treatment is under-engagement with the strongest 2026-era erosion vector.
- **Right:** named-risks section addresses AI's effect on each present Power that falls in the "exposed" list.
