## Seed entries (from build, 2026-05-18)

- **Power Progression is load-bearing.** A claim that a 2-year-old company has Process Power should fail diagnosis — Process Power requires multi-year compounding. Map every present-Power claim against the industry/company stage.
- **AI is asymmetric, not uniform.** Helmer's 2026 commentary is clear: AI erodes Switching Costs and Branding (info-asymmetry side), reinforces Cornered Resource (data) and Process Power (codification first-mover). Reject one-line dismissals of AI in moat assessment.
- **The benefit + barrier discipline catches 80% of bad Power claims.** Forcing the writer to articulate both for each present Power eliminates the vague "moat" assertions.
- **Cross-skill reconciliation is where consistency lives or dies.** value-chain-profit-pools and moat-sources name the same Helmer Power for the same industry, or one of them is wrong.
- **Roll-up archetype questions need explicit buildability framing.** "Is this Power buildable by a new entrant?" and "by a roll-up?" are different questions with different answers per Power.
