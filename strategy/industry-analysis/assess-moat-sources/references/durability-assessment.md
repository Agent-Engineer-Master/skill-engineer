# Durability Assessment — How Each Power Erodes

Every present Power must carry a durability rating with a NAMED erosion vector. "Will erode eventually" is not an assessment.

## Erosion timeframe scale

| Rating | Timeframe | Definition |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Short** | <3 years | Named erosion vector is active now or near-binding. Power is fading. |
| **Medium** | 3-7 years | Erosion vector is visible / forecastable but not yet binding. Power holds but degrades. |
| **Long** | >7 years | No credible erosion vector identified within the analysis horizon. Power is durable. |

A "Long" rating still requires an explicit statement of what HAS been considered and rejected — not silence.

## Per-Power erosion vectors

### Scale Economies — erosion signals
- Technology shift that lowers MES (e.g., cloud → smaller minimum compute footprint).
- Modularisation / disaggregation of the value chain — pieces can be assembled by sub-scale entrants.
- Demand contraction below MES for the incumbent.
- New entrant with structurally different cost curve (Counter-Positioning combo).
- **2026 AI vector:** AI lowers fixed-cost floors in software, content, customer-service. Sub-scale entrants reach MES faster.

### Network Economies — erosion signals
- Multi-homing becomes low-cost (users on multiple networks → network value flattens).
- Disintermediation — a side of the network goes direct (e.g., creators with own audience bypass platform).
- Niche networks chip away (vertical specialisation).
- Regulatory unbundling / interoperability mandates (open banking, EU DMA).

### Counter-Positioning — erosion signals
- Incumbent finally absorbs the cannibalisation cost and copies. (Watch for changes in incumbent leadership or activist pressure.)
- Entrant's model loses its structural cost/price edge as it scales (legacy creep).
- Counter-Positioning is intrinsically time-limited — typically a 5-10 year window.

### Switching Costs — erosion signals
- New integration patterns reduce migration cost (API standards, data portability mandates).
- Customer-side consolidation forces re-evaluation (M&A triggers RFP).
- **2026 AI vector:** AI agents automate schema translation, workflow re-training, and data migration. Hard-coded switching costs weaken when an LLM rewrites the integration layer.

### Branding — erosion signals
- Generational turnover — younger cohorts do not inherit brand associations automatically.
- Quality slip / brand-damaging event (recall, scandal).
- Category commoditisation — brand premium compresses.
- **2026 AI vector:** AI shopping/research agents bypass brand-led discovery. The information-asymmetry component erodes faster than the trust/safety component.

### Cornered Resource — erosion signals
- Legal expiry (patent expiry, lease end, licence renewal cycle).
- Substitute resource discovered or developed (e.g., new mining technique opening previously sub-economic deposits).
- Regulatory change (deregulation, licence pool expansion).
- Talent diaspora — key personnel leave to competitors / spin-outs.

### Process Power — erosion signals
- Process becomes codified (consulting firms or platform tooling document it) — replicability rises.
- Generational management change disrupts cultural transmission.
- Step-change in technology obsoletes the process (e.g., new manufacturing paradigm).
- **2026 AI vector:** AI can codify previously tacit process knowledge — counter-effect, AI generally REINFORCES Process Power for the first mover that uses it to codify their own routines. Net effect: depends on who codifies first.

## Required output per present Power

```
- Power: switching costs
- Rating: Medium (3-7y erosion)
- Erosion vector: AI agents automating ERP-to-ERP data migration and re-training; observed 2025 deployments in mid-market reduce switching time from ~9 to ~3 months [C: Gartner 2025 + Forrester 2024]
- Confidence: medium — vector is observed but binding-impact magnitude uncertain
```

Any present Power without all four fields fails validation.

## Cross-stage compounding

A durable Power profile usually combines:
- One **long-duration** Power as the anchor (typically Cornered Resource, Network Economies, or Process Power).
- One or more **medium-duration** Powers as reinforcers.

A Power profile composed entirely of "short" Powers is a warning sign — the winner-archetype is unstable.
