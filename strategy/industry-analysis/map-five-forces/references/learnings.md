# Learnings — map-five-forces

Behavioral feedback accumulated during real use. Update after every run via the closing feedback gate. Distinct from `edge-cases.md` which holds factual exceptions.

## What Has Worked

- Per-force "structural reason" sentence (one causal sentence after intensity) consistently produces better assessments than intensity ratings alone. Without it, drafts collapse into checklist mode.
- Requiring the governing-force sentence as a hard validator check (not just a guideline) catches universal-Moderate failure mode at ship time, not at Gate 2.
- Disaggregating supplier power by input category (specialty vs commodity) per `forces-rubric.md` consistently surfaces the structural insight that "supplier power overall is Moderate" hides.

## Patterns and Preferences

- Antony's preference (inherited from `build-company-model` work): every load-bearing claim carries a V/C/A/I tag; the lead is the analytical insight, not the data dump.
- Senior bar expectation: at least 3 non-obvious observations across the 7 forces. If the brief contains only obvious observations (e.g., "rivalry is high because there are many competitors"), it fails the bar test even if structurally complete.
- Preferred output length: ~2-3 pages of dense analysis, not 8 pages of structured emptiness. Force the analyst to choose what matters.
