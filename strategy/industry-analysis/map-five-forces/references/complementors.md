# Complementors — Sixth Force

Source: Brandenburger & Nalebuff, *Co-opetition* (1996). Reframed by BCG Henderson Institute for platform-era industries.

A **complementor** is a firm or platform whose presence INCREASES the value of this industry's product to its customers. Not a competitor, not a supplier, not a buyer.

## Examples by industry

| Industry | Complementor type | Specific examples |
|----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Mobile gaming | App stores, payment rails | Apple App Store, Google Play, Stripe |
| Electric vehicles | Charging networks, battery suppliers | ChargePoint, Tesla Supercharger, CATL |
| Medtech devices | Certification bodies, hospital IT | FDA, CE Mark, Epic, Cerner |
| Industrial robotics | Software/ML libraries, sensor makers | ROS, NVIDIA Isaac, Cognex |
| Cloud SaaS | CRM/data integrations, marketplaces | Salesforce AppExchange, Snowflake Marketplace |
| Pharma | CROs, regulatory consultancies | IQVIA, Parexel |

## Assessment

For each major complementor category, capture:

1. **Concentration** — is this complementor a monopoly/oligopoly, or fragmented?
2. **Leverage** — can the complementor extract value from the industry (e.g., app-store 30% take, payment rail interchange)?
3. **Direction of travel** — is the complementor's leverage growing, stable, or weakening?

## Output

A section in `five-forces.md` titled "Complementors (Sixth Force)":

```markdown

**Intensity:** Low | Moderate | High
**Direction:** intensifying | stable | weakening

| Complementor category | Concentration | Leverage | Direction |
|-----------------------|--------------|----------|-----------|

**Structural reason:** [one sentence on why complementors have this aggregate leverage on this industry]
```

## When complementors matter most

- Platform industries (any with two-sided market dynamics)
- Industries with mandatory certifications/standards
- Industries dependent on a critical input or distribution channel
- Industries where customer adoption requires ecosystem readiness (charging stations, app ecosystems, integration partners)

## When "none material" is acceptable

Some industries genuinely have no material complementors (e.g., bulk commodity chemicals sold direct to industrial buyers). State this explicitly with one-sentence justification — don't just omit the section.
